About Me

Born in Kitchener, Ontario, raised in Northern Alberta, and then spending a little over a decade on Vancouver Island in British Columbia, first completing a Sociology degree before entering the workforce, Amanda McLeod now resides in Gatineau, Quebec studying journalism at the University of Ottawa.

Having lived in 4 of Canada’s most populous provinces Amanda possesses a unique perspective of the Canadian landscape and the many communities that make up the collective culture. She is an accomplished traveller outside of Canada - from teaching English in China, to making wine in New Zealand, to yoga teacher training in the Himalayas of India, to surfing in the Caribbean, Amanda continually seeks new experiences in order to learn more about the world and the cultures we live in.

It is from this lens of curiosity and investigation, anchored in core values of credibility, integrity, and accuracy, that she ventures into and crafts her journalistic stories. Amanda is particularly passionate about issues that impact our natural world, dealing with the realities and effects of climate change, and with human interest stories that tell of how we connect in our communities and societies, and to our environment. As a student journalist Amanda has put together a number of stories including a multi-format piece on flooding along the Gatineau and Ottawa Rivers, as well as a piece on Ukrainian refugees and settlement agencies in the Ottawa region.

When not studying, writing, or investigating, Amanda can be found out in nature and will happily spend a day wandering through the trees trying to find a long lost lake or a rumoured berry patch.
